Disarray AI

Leads Engine - Agentic Workflow for GTM

As founding PM, I also led GTM strategy. Ahead of presenting at HumanX, I needed to turn attendance into actual customer leads — with 350+ speakers on the roster, that meant knowing who was worth approaching, whether we had a warm path to them, and what to say once we did. Doing it manually meant hours of repetitive research: pulling each person's interests, prepping personalized pitches, and cross-checking founder connections for warm intros. So I built an agentic workflow — five agents, with one human-in-the-loop step where it mattered most.

Learn more: How I did Agentic Workflow Orchestration
  1. Scout scored all 350+ speakers against explicit criteria (seniority, relevance, topic overlap), cutting the list to 48. I manually reviewed founder LinkedIn connections against that shortlist to flag warm-intro paths — that step stayed human because judging relationship strength isn't something I trusted an agent to get right, and LinkedIn's lack of a scraping-friendly API made it a non-starter anyway.
  2. Researcher pulled each person's YouTube talks and interviews from the last one to five months, so every dossier reflected what they're focused on now, not years-old content.
  3. Analyst queried each video directly with Gemini on three fixed questions: what does this person care about, what are they excited by, what are they currently working on.
  4. Editor merged the outputs into a clean, per-person dossier.
  5. QA flagged any answer that looked unsupported by the actual video for a quick manual check.

Each agent's output fed the next as structured data, so a failure at one stage never wasted the work done earlier. What would normally take a team days happened in an afternoon — going into HumanX, I had 48 warm, specific conversations queued up instead of a name badge and small talk. The pattern holds beyond GTM: find the manual step nobody questions, decide deliberately where human judgment still belongs, and let agents run the rest.
